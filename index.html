Facebook campaign objective

Engagement

✓ Recommended for TOFU

Direct response — needs warm audience and strong pixel data

Budget type

CBO — Campaign Budget Optimization

Meta distributes budget across ad sets automatically to lowest CPL

✓ Default for all BBT campaigns

ABO — Ad Set Budget

Manual control per ad set — only when split testing audiences

Use only for audience testing

Daily budget & duration

Bid strategy & attribution
Always use Highest Volume at TOFU. Never use ROAS goal, Bid Cap, or Cost-per-result goal on cold audiences — they strangle the learning phase.

Things to never touch for BBT Fitness
Block lists — reduce delivery, spike CPM. Only for national brands protecting brand safety. Not BBT.

Step 8 of 12 · Creative & Copy

Ad style & hook

Choose your content style and write the opening line. This is the most important creative decision — if they don't stop scrolling, nothing else matters.

Ad style & formula
Choose your primary influencer style, then pick the specific formula that matches what you're trying to do with this ad.

Hormozi method

Direct, bold, value-heavy. Lead with the result. Cut all fluff. Make them feel like NOT clicking is the mistake.

Best for cold Charlotte audiences · P2, P4

Dr. Shiver method

Education-first, trust-based. Lead with a problem or insight. Position BBT as the expert authority.

Best for premium programs · P3, P5, P6, P7
PAS

Problem → Agitate → Solve

Name their pain. Make it feel urgent. Present BBT as the only logical solution. Best all-around TOFU formula.

"Most women in Charlotte struggle to stay consistent…that feeling of wasting time is real…BBT fixes this with…"
AIDA

Attention → Interest → Desire → Action

Classic ad structure. Hook grabs attention, story builds interest, proof creates desire, CTA closes. Works for any funnel stage.

"Stop — this is for Charlotte women done starting over…here's what makes BBT different…join 200+ members who…"
BAB

Before → After → Bridge

Show where they are now, paint the picture of where they want to be, then position BBT as the bridge. Great for transformation ads.

"Tired of starting over every 3 weeks?…Imagine having a consistent routine…BBT's accountability system makes that real."
FAB

Feature → Advantage → Benefit

Lead with what BBT offers, explain why it's better than alternatives, land on the real-life benefit. Best for MOFU/BOFU.

"BBT uses dedicated accountability coaches [feature]…unlike group classes where no one notices if you quit [advantage]…so you actually show up [benefit]."
4P

Promise → Picture → Proof → Push

Make a bold promise, paint the future state, back it with proof, then push to action. Strong for challenge and offer-based ads.

"Transform in 6 weeks…picture showing up to summer confident…Sarah lost 14 lbs in her first challenge…claim your spot now."
STAR

Situation → Task → Action → Result

Storytelling structure. Set the scene, introduce the challenge, show what BBT did, reveal the outcome. Best for testimonial-style ads.

"Tanya was working out 5x/week and not seeing results…she needed a system not just a gym…she joined BBT S&C…down 22 lbs in 4 months."
QUEST

Qualify → Understand → Educate → Stimulate → Transition

Qualify the viewer first, show you understand their struggle, educate with an insight, stimulate with the vision, transition to CTA. Best for P3 Accountability and P7.

"If you've tried everything and still can't stay consistent, this is for you…we know how it feels…the real issue is accountability…imagine having someone…book your free call."
OATH

Oblivious → Apathetic → Thinking → Hurting

Match copy to audience awareness level. Use to decide how direct to be. Oblivious = educate. Hurting = go straight to solution. Use to calibrate TOFU vs BOFU tone.

TOFU = Oblivious audience → educate. MOFU = Thinking. BOFU = Hurting → solve immediately.
Hook — first 3–5 seconds
If they don't stop scrolling here, nothing else matters. Be specific. Use Charlotte. Use their identity, pain, or a bold claim.

After Launch · Reference

Optimization cadence

What to do after the ad goes live. This removes emotion from decisions. Follow the schedule — don't touch things early.

Day 1–3 Monitor only — do not touch

  • Kill only truly broken ads: disapproved, wrong link, wrong creative
  • Do NOT pause based on CPL yet — Meta is still in learning phase
  • Wait for at least 2,000 impressions per ad before judging CTR
  • Check: are leads coming in and hitting GHL correctly?

Day 3–5 First real optimization window

  • CTR (link) below 0.8% → replace hooks and creative immediately
  • CPL more than 40% over target → swap audience or copy, check form friction
  • If one ad set is clearly winning → shift budget toward it
  • Check show rate and book rate in GHL — leads ≠ booked calls

Week 2 Scale winners

  • Scale winning ad sets by +20–30% budget increase
  • Duplicate winning audience to a new creative set
  • Shift budget away from high CPL ad sets — don't let losers drain
  • Target CTR (link) benchmark: 0.9–1.2% minimum

Weekly ongoing Refresh and rotate

  • Refresh 1–2 creatives per week — local fatigue hits fast for brick-and-mortar
  • Rotate seasonal angles: Halloween, Thanksgiving, New Year, Summer, etc.
  • Weekly review: CPL, Book Rate (calls/leads), Show Rate, Close Rate, Cost per Sale
  • Kill / keep / iterate decision — make it in writing, not verbally
Kill rules — when to turn an ad off
CTR (link) below 0.8% after 2,000+ impressions → kill creative, replace hook
CPL more than 40% over target by Day 5 → kill ad set or swap audience
Zero leads after $50+ spend → kill and diagnose (pixel? form? landing page?)
Same creative running 4+ weeks with declining CTR → creative fatigue, replace
Scale rules — when to increase budget
CTR above 1.2% consistently → scale +20–30% budget, not more
CPL at or below target for 5+ days → duplicate to new audience
Book rate above 60% → this offer is working, push more volume
Never scale more than 30% at once — triggers learning phase reset
